Recommended by Darrian Douglas, Incoming Executive Director

This document outlines my proposed restructuring of Jazz Forum Arts' QuickBooks Online class and account structure to improve financial visibility, support grant compliance, and cleanly separate our restaurant operations from our nonprofit programming.

Based on General Ledger: October 1, 2025 – February 23, 2026  |  March 2026

Jazz Forum

Two top-level parent classes — Restaurant (commercial food & beverage operation) and Jazz Forum Arts (nonprofit/exempt purpose) — with sub-classes beneath each. The Restaurant covers food, beverage, and private events. JFA covers all programming including the Club (concerts & ticketing), education, summer series, gallery, and development. Shared costs are allocated by documented policy. Every GL account maps to exactly one class.

QBO Projects — Granular Tracking Layer

Projects sit beneath Classes and provide program/grant-level P&L without adding accounts. Every transaction tagged to a Project also carries its parent Class — so both layers report cleanly.

Implementation Action Items — Prioritized

1
CPA confirmation on Art Gallery classification — before reclassifying 411320 from Club to JFA. This has 990 and potential UBIT implications.
2
Create new sub-class hierarchy in QBO — rename "JFA" to "Jazz Forum Arts", make Club/JFA parents, create all sub-classes listed above.
3
Add two new Education GL accounts — one general income account and one general expense account to cover Lab Band, Jam Session, and Jazz Forum In School. Program-level detail lives in Projects, not the chart of accounts.
4
Create a general Venue / Site Rental expense account and retire Lyndhurst (412600). This single account covers all five summer locations, with the Summer Series class tag handling the separation from other program expenses.
5
Set up Annual Fundraiser transaction entry procedure — document the split entry process so staff correctly codes ticket revenue to Club Ticketing and donation portion to JFA Development / Unrestricted Donations at the time of entry.
6
Formalize the shared cost allocation policy in writing. Your outsourced finance firm should document this before your next 990 — the functional expense schedule requires it and auditors will ask.
7
Use Reclassify Transactions tool (QBO Accountant view) to migrate historical transactions from flat classes to new sub-class structure. Prioritize current fiscal year.
8
Retire duplicate 500000/510000/530000 expense structure — migrate any transactions and deactivate. Reduces confusion for bookkeeping staff and your outsourced firm.
9
Set up QBO Projects for active grants and new education programs — create projects for NISCA 2026, Mid-Atlantic Arts 2026, Jazz Forum In School, JFA Lab Band, Jam Session, and the Annual Fundraiser. Tag all related income and expenses at transaction entry. This replaces the need for program-specific GL accounts and gives you clean funder spending reports on demand.
